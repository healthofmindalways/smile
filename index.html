Bioavailability refers to how much of a nutrient reaches the bloodstream, where it is then absorbed by the body’s cells.

If a nutrient can’t reach the bloodstream, it’s of no use to the body.

Mark went on to say that if a drug or nutrient is given by IV, it’s considered 100% bioavailable because it goes straight into the bloodstream.

So, when you’re taking a supplement in a capsule, the bioavailability is already a lot less than 100%.
